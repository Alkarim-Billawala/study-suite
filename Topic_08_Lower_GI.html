Lower GI — Diarrhea · Malabsorption · IBD

GI II · ~2 pp
trigger → answer
Where things are absorbed (predicts the deficiency)
Duodenum: iron, calcium → celiac → iron-deficiency anemia.
Jejunum: most nutrients, folate.
Terminal ileum: B12 + bile acids → Crohn/resection → B12 deficiency + bile-acid (or fat) malabsorption.
Colon: water/electrolytes. Osmotic diarrhea stops with fasting (↑stool osmotic gap); secretory persists fasting (large volume); inflammatory = blood/pus.
★ Lower-GI emergencies
Toxic megacolon — colonic dilation >6 cm + systemic toxicity (fever, tachycardia, ↑WBC), in severe UC or C. difficile. Avoid anti-motility agents/opioids. IV fluids, steroids (if IBD)/antibiotics, decompression; surgery (colectomy) if no improvement or perforation.
EHEC O157:H7 → HUS — bloody diarrhea + microangiopathic hemolytic anemia, thrombocytopenia, AKI. Do NOT give antibiotics or anti-motility agents (↑ Shiga toxin release / HUS risk); supportive care.

Diarrhea — the approach

Acute (<2 wk) usually infectious; rehydrate
Watery/non-inflammatory norovirus, ETEC (traveler's), viral, food toxins — supportive ± loperamide OK
Bloody/inflammatory Shigella, Salmonella, Campylobacter, EHEC, C. diff, Entamoeba — avoid loperamide, stool studies
Chronic (>4 wk) osmotic (fasting-responsive), secretory (large-volume, persists fasting), inflammatory (calprotectin↑, scope), fatty/steatorrhea
Overflow elderly + constipation/impaction presenting as "diarrhea"

Malabsorption

Celiac gluten → duodenal villous atrophy; iron-def anemia, dermatitis herpetiformis; tTG-IgA + total IgA on gluten → biopsy; assoc T1DM/thyroid/IgA def; ↑lymphoma; lifelong GFD
Lactose intolerance osmotic diarrhea/bloating after dairy
SIBO bloating; low B12, high folate; breath test; antibiotics
Bile-acid malabsorption ileal disease/resection → watery diarrhea → cholestyramine
Pancreatic insufficiency chronic pancreatitis/CF → steatorrhea, ↓fecal elastase → enzyme replacement

IBD — work-up & treatment

Work-up fecal calprotectin, colonoscopy + biopsy, cross-sectional imaging (Crohn)
5-ASA mild UC induction/maintenance
Corticosteroids induction ONLY — not maintenance (osteoporosis, adrenal suppression, infection, AVN)
Immunomodulators azathioprine/6-MP, methotrexate (maintenance)
Biologics anti-TNF (infliximab/adalimumab), vedolizumab, ustekinumab — screen TB/HepB first
Surgery UC → curative colectomy; Crohn → for strictures/fistulae/abscess (recurs)

IBS · C. difficile · pitfalls

IBS (Rome IV)
Criteria recurrent abdominal pain ≥1 day/wk ×3 mo + ≥2 of (defecation-related, change in frequency, change in form); no alarm features
Treat low-FODMAP, fiber, antispasmodics; loperamide (IBS-D), laxatives/linaclotide (IBS-C), TCA/SSRI
C. difficile
Risk recent antibiotics (clindamycin, fluoroquinolones, cephalosporins), healthcare exposure
Dx/Tx stool toxin/PCR; oral vancomycin or fidaxomicin, stop offending antibiotic; recurrent → fecal transplant
Pitfalls
No antibiotics in EHEC (HUS risk); no loperamide in bloody diarrhea
Calprotectin separates inflammatory (IBD) from functional (IBS)
Steroids ≠ maintenance in IBD
