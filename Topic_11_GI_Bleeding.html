GI Bleeding

spans GI I + GI IV (upper & lower)
trigger → answer
Localize by what comes out — but resuscitate first
Hematemesis / coffee-ground = upper. Melena (black, tarry) = usually upper (blood digested). Hematochezia (bright/maroon) = usually lower — but a brisk upper bleed can present as hematochezia in an unstable patient.
BUN:creatinine ↑ favors upper (digested blood = protein load).
Always first: ABCs, two large-bore IVs, crystalloid, type & cross, monitor; the resuscitation precedes the diagnosis.
Risk scores: Glasgow-Blatchford (pre-endoscopy; identifies very-low-risk for outpatient care), Rockall (mortality/rebleed).
★ Massive / unstable bleed
Resuscitate to endoscopy: 2 large IVs, balanced crystalloid, restrictive transfusion (Hb threshold ~70, ~80 if cardiac), platelets if <50 + bleeding, FFP/PCC + reverse anticoagulation. Erythromycin/NG before scope to clear the stomach. Urgent endoscopy when stabilized.
Suspected variceal bleed (known cirrhosis/portal HTN): add octreotide + ceftriaxone + PPI → urgent band ligation; balloon tamponade or TIPS as rescue. Over-transfusion raises portal pressure → rebleed.

Resuscitation & assessment

Airway/Breathing/Circulation protect airway if hematemesis/altered; 2 large-bore IVs
Transfuse restrictive Hb ~70 (improves survival vs liberal); platelets <50 + bleeding; FFP/PCC for coagulopathy
Reverse hold/reverse anticoagulants & antiplatelets per bleeding severity
Stratify Glasgow-Blatchford (triage), Rockall (prognosis)
Prep scope IV erythromycin / NG lavage to improve visualization

Upper GI bleed

Causes peptic ulcer #1, esophagogastric varices, Mallory-Weiss, erosive gastritis/esophagitis, malignancy, Dieulafoy, AVM, aortoenteric fistula (prior graft)
Manage (non-variceal) high-dose IV PPI → endoscopy within 24 h; endoscopic hemostasis (clips/thermal/injection); Forrest class predicts rebleed
After H. pylori test & treat, stop NSAIDs
Variceal octreotide + ceftriaxone + band ligation (see emergencies)

Lower GI bleed

Causes diverticulosis #1 (painless), angiodysplasia (elderly; Heyde = AS + angiodysplasia), hemorrhoids, ischemic colitis, neoplasm/polyp, IBD, post-polypectomy, infectious colitis
Work-up (stable) colonoscopy after prep
Work-up (unstable/massive) CT angiography → embolization, or surgery; tagged RBC scan for slow bleeds
⚠ if unstable hematochezia, consider an upper source (do EGD) — brisk UGIB mimics LGIB

Variceal bleeding (focused)

Acute octreotide (splanchnic vasoconstriction) + ceftriaxone (improves survival) + PPI → band ligation
Rescue balloon tamponade (Sengstaken-Blakemore), TIPS
Prophylaxis non-selective β-blocker (propranolol/nadolol/carvedilol) and/or EVL — not for the acute bleed
Transfusion restrictive — over-transfusion ↑portal pressure → rebleed

Obscure / occult bleeding

Occult positive FOBT/FIT or iron-deficiency anemia with normal initial scopes
IDA in older adult colonoscopy + EGD to exclude malignancy
Obscure (negative scopes) small-bowel source → capsule endoscopy; angiodysplasia common

Pitfalls

Melena treat as upper until proven otherwise
Unstable hematochezia rule out a brisk upper source (EGD)
Restrictive transfusion Hb ~70 — liberal worsens outcomes (esp. varices)
Ceftriaxone in cirrhotic GI bleed improves survival — don't omit it
Aortoenteric fistula consider in anyone with a prior aortic graft + GI bleed
