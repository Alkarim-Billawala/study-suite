Acute Abdomen & GI Surgery

GI III · ~2 pp
trigger → answer
Reading abdominal pain
Referred pain by embryonic gut: foregut → epigastric, midgut → periumbilical, hindgut → suprapubic. Pain that migrates & localizes (visceral → parietal) signals peritoneal irritation (e.g., appendicitis).
Peritonism = rigidity, rebound, guarding, pain on movement/cough → surgical abdomen. Pain out of proportion to a soft exam → mesenteric ischemia.
Always: resuscitate, NPO, analgesia (doesn't mask the exam), labs + lactate, β-hCG in women, erect CXR/CT.
"Surgery now" triggers: free air/perforation, generalized peritonitis, ischemia, closed-loop obstruction, uncontrolled bleeding, septic source needing control.
★ The acute-abdomen emergencies
Perforation / peritonitis — rigid abdomen + free air → resuscitate, broad antibiotics, urgent laparotomy.
Intra-abdominal sepsis/septic shock — resuscitate + early antibiotics + source control (drain/operate); don't delay for perfect imaging in refractory shock.
Acute mesenteric ischemia — pain out of proportion, lactate↑; CT angiography; emergent revascularization/resection. Suspect with AF (embolic).

Appendicitis

Classic periumbilical pain → migrates to RLQ (McBurney), anorexia, nausea, low fever
Signs rebound, Rovsing, psoas, obturator
Dx clinical + CT/US (US in young/pregnant); appendicolith
Treat laparoscopic appendectomy + antibiotics; complicated (abscess) → antibiotics ± drainage, interval appendectomy

Acute pancreatitis

Causes gallstones #1, alcohol #2, hypertriglyceridemia, hypercalcemia, ERCP, drugs (GET SMASHED)
Diagnose (2 of 3) epigastric pain → back · lipase >3× ULN · imaging
Severity organ failure, CRP, BISAP/APACHE; CT for necrosis
Manage aggressive IV fluids, analgesia, early enteral feeding; treat cause (ERCP if cholangitis/obstruction; chole same admission for gallstones)
⚠ no prophylactic antibiotics — only infected necrosis. Complications: necrosis, pseudocyst, ARDS, hypocalcemia

Bowel obstruction

SBO adhesions #1, hernias, tumor; colicky pain, bilious vomiting, distension, obstipation; dilated small bowel + air-fluid levels
SBO management NPO, IV fluids, electrolytes, NG decompression, serial exams; surgery if strangulation/closed loop/ischemia/peritonitis
LBO colorectal cancer #1, sigmoid volvulus (coffee-bean → endoscopic detorsion), stricture
Ogilvie colonic pseudo-obstruction (no mechanical cause) → supportive, decompression, neostigmine after excluding obstruction
Strangulation = operate
Red flags constant pain, fever, tachycardia, peritonism, ↑lactate, leukocytosis

Diverticulitis & mesenteric ischemia

Diverticulitis
Picture LLQ pain, fever, leukocytosis; CT diagnostic (Hinchey stage)
Manage bowel rest ± selective antibiotics; abscess → drain; perforation → surgery; avoid acute colonoscopy → scope at ~6 wk to exclude cancer
Mesenteric ischemia
Acute (small bowel, SMA) embolic (AF), thrombotic, NOMI (shock/pressors), venous (hypercoagulable); pain out of proportion, acidosis → CT angio, emergency
Chronic postprandial pain, food fear, weight loss ("intestinal angina")
Ischemic colitis elderly, watershed (splenic flexure), crampy pain then bloody diarrhea; usually supportive; surgery if gangrene

Anorectal · hernias · perioperative

Internal hemorrhoids painless BRBPR, grade I–IV; fiber/banding/surgery; bleeding is dx of exclusion — rule out CRC
Anal fissure severe pain with defecation, posterior midline; fiber, topical CCB/nitrate, sitz baths
Perianal abscess fluctuant, painful → incision & drainage
Hernia reducible → incarcerated (irreducible) → strangulated (ischemic, surgical emergency); femoral higher strangulation risk
Consent capacity + risks/benefits/alternatives incl. no treatment
Preop optimize anemia (IV iron), cardiac risk, anticoagulation; VTE + antibiotic prophylaxis

Colorectal cancer & pitfalls

CRC adenoma→carcinoma; right = anemia/occult bleed, left = obstruction/caliber change; colonoscopy + biopsy, staging CT, CEA → resection (hemicolectomy) ± adjuvant
Pain out of proportion mesenteric ischemia until proven otherwise
Don't scope acute diverticulitis (perforation)
IDA / BRBPR never blame hemorrhoids until CRC excluded
Free air / rigid abdomen → OR, not more imaging
