Chronic Kidney Disease & Complications

KU 1 · ~2 pp
trigger → answer
Definition, staging & causes
CKD: eGFR <60 or markers of kidney damage (albuminuria, hematuria, structural) for ≥3 months.
GFR stages: G1 ≥90 (+damage), G2 60–89, G3a 45–59, G3b 30–44, G4 15–29, G5 <15 (ESRD). Albuminuria: A1 <3, A2 3–30, A3 >30 mg/mmol.
Causes: diabetes #1, hypertension #2, glomerulonephritis, ADPKD (flank pain, hematuria, HTN, hepatic cysts, berry aneurysms), obstructive.
CV disease is the #1 cause of death in CKD — CKD is a cardiovascular risk multiplier.

Slowing progression

Blood pressure target ~<130/80
ACEi / ARB first-line with albuminuria (↓intraglomerular pressure, ↓proteinuria); accept ≤30% Cr rise, monitor K⁺
SGLT2 inhibitor slows progression in diabetic AND non-diabetic CKD (dapagliflozin/empagliflozin)
Also glycemic control, sodium/protein moderation, avoid nephrotoxins (NSAIDs, contrast)

CKD-MBD (mineral-bone)

Mechanism ↓1-α-hydroxylase → ↓1,25-vit D; phosphate retention; ↑FGF23 → ↓Ca → secondary hyperPTH
Lab pattern ↓Ca, ↑phosphate, ↑PTH, ↓1,25-vit D
Treat dietary phosphate restriction + phosphate binders (non-calcium e.g. sevelamer) + active vit D (calcitriol) + cinacalcet (lowers PTH & Ca) → prevents renal osteodystrophy

Anemia of CKD

Mechanism EPO deficiency → normocytic normochromic anemia
First ensure iron repletion before starting an ESA
ESA target Hb ~100–115 g/L (don't normalize >130 — ↑stroke/CV risk)

Other complications

Metabolic acidosis ↓ammonia excretion → treat with oral sodium bicarbonate (slows progression, protects bone/muscle)
Hyperkalemia ↓excretion; diet, binders; balance against ACEi/ARB benefit
Volume / HTN salt restriction, diuretics
Uremia (late) pericarditis, encephalopathy, platelet dysfunction, pruritus, restless legs
CV disease leading cause of death — aggressive risk-factor control

Kidney replacement

When (AEIOU) refractory Acidosis, Electrolytes (hyperK), Intoxication, Overload, Uremia — or progressive ESRD with symptoms
Access create AV fistula early (preferred over catheter); "fistula first"
Modalities hemodialysis, peritoneal dialysis
Transplant best survival/quality of life when eligible

Pitfalls

eGFR equations assume steady state — invalid in AKI/non-steady state
ACEi/ARB expect ≤30% Cr bump; stop if larger or hyperK; contraindicated in bilateral renal artery stenosis and pregnancy
Don't over-correct anemia Hb >130 increases harm
CKD = CV risk equivalent treat lipids/BP aggressively
ADPKD screen for berry aneurysm if family history of subarachnoid hemorrhage
