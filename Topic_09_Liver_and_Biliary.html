Liver & Biliary

GI III + GI IV · ~2–3 pp
trigger → answer
Reading LFTs & jaundice
Hepatocellular = ↑↑ALT/AST; cholestatic = ↑↑ALP/GGT. Isolated ↑ALP → check GGT to confirm it's liver (vs bone).
Synthetic function: ↑INR is the earliest marker of acute failure; ↓albumin reflects chronicity. AST:ALT >2:1 → alcohol; values in the thousands → ischemia/toxin/virus.
Unconjugated (indirect) hyperbilirubinemia: hemolysis, Gilbert, ineffective erythropoiesis — no bilirubin in urine.
Conjugated (direct): hepatocellular or obstructive — dark urine; pale stools + dark urine = obstruction.
★ Hepatobiliary emergencies
Variceal hemorrhage — resuscitate + airway → octreotide + ceftriaxone + PPI → urgent band ligation; balloon tamponade/TIPS as rescue. (β-blocker is prophylaxis, not for the acute bleed.)
Ascending cholangitis — Charcot (fever/RUQ/jaundice) ± Reynolds (hypotension/AMS) → resuscitate + broad antibiotics + urgent ERCP decompression.
Acute liver failure — encephalopathy + coagulopathy (no prior cirrhosis); acetaminophen #1 → NAC; King's College criteria for transplant.

Cirrhosis & complications

Portal HTN varices, splenomegaly, ascites, caput medusae
Ascites — SAAG ≥11 = portal HTN (transudate); <11 = malignancy/infection (exudate). Treat: Na restriction + spironolactone + furosemide; LVP + albumin; refractory → TIPS
SBP ascitic PMN >250 → cefotaxime + albumin (prevent HRS); prophylaxis norfloxacin
Hepatic encephalopathy ammonia; precipitants (GI bleed, infection, constipation, electrolytes, sedatives); asterixis = stage 2; lactulose + rifaximin
Hepatorenal syndrome functional AKI; albumin + vasoconstrictor (terlipressin); transplant
HCC surveillance US ± AFP q6mo in cirrhosis

Specific liver diseases

Alcoholic hepatitis AST:ALT >2:1; Maddrey ≥32 → prednisolone (not if active infection/GIB)
NAFLD/NASH metabolic syndrome; commonest chronic liver disease → weight loss
Autoimmune hepatitis young ♀, ↑IgG, ANA/ASMA (I) or anti-LKM1 (II) → steroids + azathioprine
PBC middle-aged ♀, pruritus, cholestatic, AMA, ↑IgM → UDCA
PSC ♂ + IBD/UC, beaded ducts on MRCP, p-ANCA; ↑cholangiocarcinoma
Hemochromatosis HFE C282Y; ↑ferritin/transferrin sat; bronze diabetes, cardiomyopathy, arthropathy → phlebotomy
Wilson ATP7B; ↓ceruloplasmin, KF rings, neuropsych, hemolysis, young → chelation/zinc
A1AT deficiency liver disease + panacinar emphysema

Gallstone disease — the spectrum

Risk cholesterol stones: female, fat, forty, fertile; pigment: hemolysis
Biliary colic postprandial RUQ pain <6 h, no fever, normal labs → elective lap chole
Acute cholecystitis >6 h, fever, Murphy+, leukocytosis, US wall thickening → abx + early lap chole
Choledocholithiasis jaundice, dilated CBD, ↑bilirubin/ALP → ERCP then chole
Ascending cholangitis Charcot/Reynolds → urgent ERCP (see emergencies)
Gallstone pancreatitis ↑lipase; supportive; cholecystectomy same admission
Courvoisier painless jaundice + palpable non-tender gallbladder → malignancy (pancreatic/cholangio)

Pitfalls

Window period only anti-HBc IgM positive (HBsAg gone, anti-HBs not yet up)
SAAG direction ≥11 portal HTN; <11 exudate — easy to invert
β-blocker prophylaxis only — use octreotide for the acute variceal bleed
Acetaminophen even "therapeutic" doses can injure malnourished/alcoholic livers
Transplant active alcohol/substance use = absolute contraindication
Confirm SBP paracentesis before committing to long antibiotic course
