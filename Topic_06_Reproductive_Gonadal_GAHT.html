Reproductive / Gonadal & GAHT

Endo III · ~2 pp
trigger → answer
HPG axis & the rule that explains the labs
Axis: hypothalamic GnRH (pulsatile) → pituitary LH/FSH → gonads (testosterone/estradiol, inhibin). Pulsatile stimulates; continuous SUPPRESSES — basis of GnRH-agonist downregulation (puberty blockers, prostate Ca).
Localize by gonadotropins: gonad failing → low feedback → ↑LH/FSH (hypergonadotropic = primary). Pituitary/hypothalamic failure → ↓/normal LH/FSH (hypogonadotropic = secondary).
Menstrual cycle: follicular (FSH → estradiol rises) → LH surge → ovulation → luteal (corpus luteum → progesterone; day-21 progesterone confirms ovulation).
Androgen sources (women): ovary + adrenal. ↑↑DHEAS → adrenal source; ↑↑testosterone with normal DHEAS → ovarian.
★ Hypogonadism — read LH/FSH to localize

PCOS & hyperandrogenism (♀)

Rotterdam (2 of 3) oligo/anovulation · clinical/biochemical hyperandrogenism · polycystic ovaries on US
Diagnosis of exclusion check TSH, prolactin, 17-OHP (NCAH), Cushing if features, testosterone/DHEAS if rapid/severe (tumor)
Associations ↑LH:FSH, insulin resistance, metabolic syndrome; endometrial hyperplasia/cancer from unopposed estrogen
Treat lifestyle/weight; OCP (cycle control, hirsutism, endometrial protection) ± spironolactone; metformin (metabolic); letrozole 1st-line for ovulation induction
Red flag → think tumour, not PCOS
Rapid virilization very high T (ovarian) or very high DHEAS (adrenal), clitoromegaly, voice change → image, don't label PCOS

Amenorrhea (♀)

Definitions primary: no menarche by 15 (with 2° sexual chars) or 13 (without); secondary: ≥3 missed cycles
Step 1 always pregnancy test; then FSH/LH, prolactin, TSH, estradiol
↑FSH/LH primary ovarian — Turner, POI, gonadal dysgenesis
↓FSH/LH functional hypothalamic amenorrhea (stress/exercise/low energy availability), hyperprolactinemia, Kallmann, pituitary
Anatomic/outflow imperforate hymen, Müllerian agenesis (MRKH), Asherman, CAIS

Male hypogonadism & infertility

Symptoms ↓libido, ED, fatigue, ↓muscle/energy, gynecomastia, infertility, osteoporosis
Confirm morning total testosterone ×2 → then LH/FSH to localize; add prolactin, iron studies (hemochromatosis)
Klinefelter tall, small firm testes, gynecomastia, ↑LH/FSH, ↓T, azoospermia
Semen analysis volume/concentration/motility/morphology; severe oligo/azoospermia → karyotype, Y-microdeletion, CFTR (CBAVD)
⚠ TRT suppresses spermatogenesis — never for fertility; use gonadotropins/clomiphene instead. Also causes polycythemia.

Differences of sex development

5α-reductase deficiency 46,XY; ↓DHT → male internal, undervirilized external; virilize at puberty
CAIS 46,XY; androgen-receptor defect → female phenotype, testes, NO uterus (MIF intact), primary amenorrhea, scant pubic hair
CAH (21-OH) 46,XX virilization / ambiguous genitalia; ↑17-OHP (see Adrenal)
Turner 45,X short stature, webbed neck, streak gonads, coarctation, primary amenorrhea

Menopause & hormone therapy

Labs/picture ↑FSH, ↓estradiol; vasomotor symptoms, genitourinary syndrome, bone loss
HRT — who symptomatic, age <60 or <10 yr from menopause
Add a progestogen if uterus intact (prevents endometrial hyperplasia/cancer)
Transdermal preferred if HTN, VTE risk, migraine (avoids hepatic first-pass)
Vaginal estrogen for genitourinary symptoms only
Contraindications breast cancer, VTE/CAD, undiagnosed vaginal bleeding

GAHT & pitfalls

Gender-affirming hormone therapy
Feminizing estradiol (transdermal preferred) + antiandrogen: spironolactone (→ hyperkalemia) or GnRH agonist; cyproterone (→ ↑prolactin/hepatotox). Monitor estradiol, T, K⁺, prolactin; VTE risk.
Masculinizing testosterone → monitor hematocrit (polycythemia), lipids, BP; amenorrhea expected
Puberty blockers GnRH agonists (continuous → downregulation)
Counsel fertility before starting (gamete preservation)
Pitfalls
Pulsatile vs continuous GnRH continuous = suppression (a frequent trick)
Pregnancy first in any amenorrhea work-up
Spironolactone teratogenic + hyperkalemia — counsel contraception
