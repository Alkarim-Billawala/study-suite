Thyroid

spans Endo II + Endo IV · ~2 pp
trigger → answer
Physiology you must have to reason through the rest
Axis: hypothalamic TRH → pituitary TSH → thyroid T4 (& some T3). T4/T3 feed back to suppress TSH. Negative feedback is why TSH is the most sensitive screen.
T4 = prohormone; T3 = active. Peripheral 5′-deiodinase converts T4→T3 (mostly liver/kidney). Illness shunts to inactive reverse T3.
Synthesis: iodide trapped → organified onto thyroglobulin by thyroid peroxidase (TPO) → coupling. Thionamides block TPO; PTU also blocks peripheral T4→T3.
Wolff–Chaikoff: a large iodine load transiently shuts off synthesis (autoregulation) → basis for giving iodine in storm + amiodarone hypothyroidism.
Jod–Basedow: iodine load triggers hyperthyroidism in an autonomous nodular gland → why iodine is given after a thionamide in storm.
Binding proteins (TBG): estrogen/pregnancy ↑TBG → ↑total T4 but free normal; nephrotic syndrome/androgens ↓TBG. Always interpret free hormone.

★ Thyroid emergencies
Thyroid storm — fever, AF/SVT, agitation→delirium, vomiting/diarrhea, heart failure; precipitated by infection, surgery, trauma, iodine/contrast, RAI, or stopping thionamides. Dx is clinical (Burch–Wartofsky). Treatment order matters:
① β-blocker (propranolol — also blunts T4→T3) ② PTU (blocks synthesis + peripheral conversion) ③ iodine (Lugol/SSKI) ≥1 h AFTER PTU (give synthesis-block first, then block release) ④ hydrocortisone (blocks conversion, covers relative adrenal insufficiency) ⑤ cooling, fluids, treat trigger.
Myxedema coma — decompensated hypothyroidism: hypothermia, ↓LOC/coma, hypoventilation (CO₂ retention), bradycardia, hyponatremia, hypoglycemia; often elderly woman + cold/infection trigger. Treatment: IV levothyroxine (± T3) + IV hydrocortisone FIRST (cover concurrent adrenal insufficiency before thyroid hormone) + passive rewarming + ventilatory/fluid support + treat trigger. High mortality.

Hypothyroidism

Primary causes Hashimoto (anti-TPO) #1 in iodine-replete areas; iodine deficiency #1 worldwide; post-RAI/thyroidectomy; drugs (amiodarone, lithium, checkpoint inhibitors); subacute thyroiditis phase
Central pituitary/hypothalamic disease; ↓TSH + ↓fT4 — monitor by free T4
Clinical
Classic fatigue, cold intolerance, weight gain, constipation, dry skin, hair loss, bradycardia, delayed-relaxation reflexes, menorrhagia
Easy to miss hyponatremia, ↑LDL, ↑CK/myalgia, macrocytic anemia, carpal tunnel, hoarseness, pericardial/pleural effusions, non-pitting (myxedema) edema
Treatment
Levothyroxine ~1.6 µg/kg/day; elderly/CAD start low (25–50 µg, titrate) to avoid angina/arrhythmia
Administration empty stomach, 30–60 min before food; separate from Ca, iron, PPI, coffee (↓absorption)
↑ requirement pregnancy, estrogen, malabsorption, rifampin/phenytoin/carbamazepine
⚠ Before starting screen for adrenal insufficiency — levothyroxine alone can precipitate adrenal crisis
Monitor TSH at 6–8 wk (central: free T4)

Hyperthyroidism / Thyrotoxicosis

Classic weight loss + ↑appetite, heat intolerance, palpitations/AF, fine tremor, anxiety, diarrhea, brisk reflexes, sweating, osteoporosis, proximal myopathy
Apathetic (elderly) may present only with AF, weight loss, depression, failure to thrive
The pivot: radioactive iodine uptake (RAIU)
High & diffuse Graves disease
Single hot focus toxic adenoma
Multiple patchy hot foci toxic multinodular goiter
LOW / absent uptake destructive thyroiditis, exogenous/factitious intake, iodine load, struma ovarii (ectopic) — hormone released, not synthesized
Graves specifics
Confirm TRAb/TSI positive; diffuse goiter ± bruit
Extrathyroidal orbitopathy (proptosis/diplopia — worse with smoking, high TRAb, and RAI), pretibial myxedema, thyroid acropachy

Hyperthyroidism — treatment options

OptionKey points / cautions
β-blockersymptom control immediately; propranolol also blunts T4→T3. Bridge for all causes incl. thyroiditis.
Methimazolefirst-line thionamide (Graves): once-daily, potent. Teratogenic 1st trimester (aplasia cutis, embryopathy). SE: agranulocytosis, cholestatic hepatitis, rash.
PTUblocks peripheral conversion → preferred in storm & 1st trimester. Higher (fulminant) hepatotoxicity — black box; 2–3× daily.
Radioactive iodinedefinitive; causes hypothyroidism. Contraindicated in pregnancy/breastfeeding; can worsen orbitopathy (cover with steroids).
Surgeryfor large/compressive goiter, suspected malignancy, severe orbitopathy, or drug-intolerant pregnancy. Risks: hypoparathyroidism, recurrent laryngeal nerve injury.
Before any thionamide baseline CBC + LFTs; warn: stop & check CBC if fever/sore throat (agranulocytosis, usually first 90 days)

Thyroiditis

All destructive forms LOW uptake; often triphasic: thyrotoxic → hypothyroid → euthyroid; thyrotoxic phase treated with β-blocker ONLY (no thionamides)
Subacute granulomatous (de Quervain) PAINFUL tender goiter, post-viral, ↑↑ESR/CRP → NSAIDs, steroids if severe
Silent / postpartum PAINLESS, autoimmune (anti-TPO+), within 12 mo of delivery; ~20–50% → permanent hypothyroid; recurs in future pregnancies; annual TSH
Hashimoto chronic lymphocytic; anti-TPO; ends hypothyroid (early transient "hashitoxicosis"); risk of thyroid lymphoma
Drug-induced amiodarone, lithium, interferon, immune checkpoint inhibitors
Riedel woody-hard fixed gland; IgG4-related fibrosis

Nodule & thyroid cancer

Work-up — TSH directs the next test
Suppressed TSH → radionuclide scan; a hot (functioning) nodule is almost never malignant → treat the hyperthyroidism, no FNA
Normal / high TSH → ultrasound; FNA based on size + sonographic risk (microcalcifications, hypoechoic, irregular margins, taller-than-wide, abnormal cervical nodes). Cytology reported by Bethesda category.

Special situations & pitfalls

Amiodarone (≈40% iodine by weight)
Hypothyroidism (AIH) common, via Wolff-Chaikoff → levothyroxine; usually continue amiodarone
AIT type 1 iodine-induced on nodular/Graves gland (↑uptake/vascularity) → thionamide ± perchlorate
AIT type 2 destructive thyroiditis (↓uptake) → glucocorticoids
Pregnancy
hCG mimics TSH gestational thyrotoxicosis / hyperemesis gravidarum — self-limited, no antithyroid drug
Antithyroid PTU 1st trimester → switch to methimazole 2nd/3rd; RAI contraindicated
Hypothyroid levothyroxine need rises early — increase dose ~30%, trimester-specific TSH targets
Assay & lab traps
Biotin supplements interfere with immunoassays → falsely ↓TSH + ↑T4 (mimics Graves); hold biotin before testing
TBG shifts estrogen/pregnancy ↑total T4 (free normal); read free hormone
Don't screen in acute illness sick-euthyroid changes resolve with recovery
