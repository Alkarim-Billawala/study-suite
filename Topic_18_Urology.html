Urology

KU 2–3 · ~2 pp
trigger → answer
★ Urologic emergencies
Testicular torsion — sudden severe pain, high-riding/horizontal testis, absent cremasteric reflex, negative Prehn → urgent surgical exploration within ~6 h. Don't delay for imaging.
Obstructing stone + infection (fever, obstruction on imaging) → urosepsis → emergency decompression (ureteric stent or percutaneous nephrostomy) + antibiotics.
Fournier gangrene — necrotizing fasciitis of the perineum (diabetics) → resuscitation, broad antibiotics, urgent surgical debridement.

BPH & LUTS

Pathology transition-zone enlargement → bladder outlet obstruction
Symptoms voiding (hesitancy, weak stream, intermittency, incomplete emptying) + storage (frequency, urgency, nocturia); IPSS
Complications retention, UTI, stones, hydronephrosis/post-renal AKI
α-blocker (tamsulosin) rapid relief; SE orthostatic hypotension (additive with antihypertensives), retrograde ejaculation, floppy-iris
5α-reductase inhibitor (finasteride) shrinks large glands over ~6 mo; ↓libido/ED, halves PSA, teratogenic
Refractory TURP; acute retention → catheter (see AKI: decompress at the outlet)

Prostate cancer

Pathology peripheral-zone adenocarcinoma; hard nodule on DRE, ↑PSA
Spread osteoblastic bone metastases → back pain
Grade Gleason score / grade groups
Screening PSA — shared decision-making (controversial)
Localized active surveillance (low risk), radical prostatectomy, or radiation
Metastatic androgen deprivation (GnRH agonist/antagonist ± antiandrogen); cover agonist flare with antiandrogen

Testicular: the three-way split

Torsion sudden, severe, horizontal lie, absent cremasteric, negative Prehn → surgery (emergency)
Epididymitis gradual, fever, dysuria, positive Prehn (relief on elevation); young = STI (GC/chlamydia), older = coliform → antibiotics
Tumor painless hard mass, young men, cryptorchidism risk → US + AFP/β-hCG/LDH → radical inguinal orchiectomy; NEVER trans-scrotal biopsy

Nephrolithiasis

Stone types calcium oxalate (most common); uric acid (radiolucent, acidic urine); struvite (infection/Proteus → staghorn); cystine
Presentation acute renal colic (flank → groin), hematuria, nausea
Diagnose non-contrast CT (gold standard); US
Manage hydration, analgesia (NSAIDs), medical expulsive therapy (α-blocker) for distal stones <10 mm; most <5 mm pass
Admit / urgent urology
If obstruction + infection (decompress!), solitary kidney, AKI, intractable pain/vomiting, large stone
Prevent hydration; thiazide (calcium stones), citrate, allopurinol (uric acid)

UTI & prostatitis

Cystitis dysuria, frequency, urgency → nitrofurantoin / TMP-SMX / fosfomycin
Pyelonephritis fever, flank pain, CVA tenderness → fluoroquinolone or IV; image if obstruction suspected
Complicated men, pregnancy, catheter, obstruction, immunocompromise
Acute bacterial prostatitis fever, dysuria, tender boggy prostate — don't massage; E. coli → fluoroquinolone/TMP-SMX
Asymptomatic bacteriuria treat ONLY in pregnancy or before urologic procedures

Hematuria, bladder Ca & incontinence

Painless gross hematuria (older/smoker) urothelial bladder cancer until proven → CT urogram + cystoscopy + cytology
Glomerular vs urologic dysmorphic RBCs/casts/protein = glomerular; isomorphic = urologic
Stress leak with cough/exertion → pelvic floor exercises, surgery
Urge / OAB detrusor overactivity → antimuscarinic (oxybutynin — contraindicated in narrow-angle glaucoma), mirabegron, behavioral
Overflow retention/obstruction or neurogenic → catheter, treat cause

Pitfalls

Torsion operate — don't wait for ultrasound
Testicular mass US + markers; never trans-scrotal biopsy (seeding)
Stone + infection emergency decompression, not just antibiotics
GnRH agonist flare cover with antiandrogen in prostate cancer
α-blocker orthostatic hypotension stacked on antihypertensives
PDE5 inhibitors contraindicated with nitrates
