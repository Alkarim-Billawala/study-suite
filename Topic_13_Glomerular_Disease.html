★ Glomerular emergency — RPGN
Rapidly progressive GN = nephritic picture (RBC casts) with creatinine climbing over days–weeks; crescents on biopsy. Three immune patterns: anti-GBM (linear IgG), immune-complex (granular — lupus, post-strep, IgA), pauci-immune (ANCA). Start empiric IV pulse corticosteroids ± cyclophosphamide urgently — do NOT wait for biopsy; add plasmapheresis for anti-GBM.

Nephrotic syndrome

Definition proteinuria >3.5 g/day + hypoalbuminemia + edema + hyperlipidemia/lipiduria (Maltese crosses)
Complications hypercoagulable (loss of antithrombin III → renal vein thrombosis/PE), infection (Ig loss), hyperlipidemia
Manage ACEi/ARB (↓proteinuria), diuretics, statin, treat cause; anticoagulate if indicated

Nephrotic causes

Minimal change children; foot-process effacement on EM; steroid-responsive; idiopathic / NSAIDs / Hodgkin
FSGS commonest in adults (esp. Black ancestry); HIV, obesity, reflux; segmental sclerosis
Membranous anti-PLA2R; "spike and dome" subepithelial deposits; secondary (malignancy, hep B, SLE, drugs); very hypercoagulable
Diabetic Kimmelstiel-Wilson nodules; commonest overall
Amyloid Congo red, apple-green birefringence

Nephritic syndrome

Picture hematuria (dysmorphic RBCs, RBC casts), HTN, oliguria, ↓GFR, sub-nephrotic proteinuria, edema
Key reflex check complement to split the differential

Nephritic causes — by complement

LOW complement post-streptococcal (↓C3, 1–3 wk post-GAS, subepithelial humps, resolves), MPGN, lupus nephritis (↓C3/C4, anti-dsDNA), cryoglobulinemia (hep C), endocarditis
NORMAL complement IgA nephropathy (commonest GN; synpharyngitic hematuria 1–2 d after URI), IgA vasculitis/HSP, ANCA-associated (pauci-immune), anti-GBM/Goodpasture (hemoptysis)
IgA vs post-strep (classic discriminator)
IgA hematuria with/1–2 d after URI, normal complement
Post-strep hematuria 1–3 wk after, ↓C3, ↑ASO

The vasculitides (pulmonary-renal)

Anti-GBM / Goodpasture linear IgG, hemoptysis + RBC casts, anti-GBM Ab → plasmapheresis + steroids + cyclophosphamide
GPA c-ANCA/PR3; sinus + lung + kidney
MPA p-ANCA/MPO; lung + kidney
EGPA asthma + eosinophilia + p-ANCA
All ANCA pauci-immune, normal complement

Work-up & pitfalls

Work-up urinalysis/microscopy, protein quantification (UACR/PCR), serologies (ANA, dsDNA, complement, ANCA, anti-GBM, ASO, hep B/C, cryoglobulins), biopsy
Pitfalls
Casts RBC = nephritic; fatty/oval-fat = nephrotic
Membranous screen for malignancy/secondary causes; high VTE risk
RPGN renal emergency — treat empirically before biopsy
Normal complement + hemoptysis think ANCA or anti-GBM (not lupus)
