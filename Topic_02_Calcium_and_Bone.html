Physiology — the two hormones that move calcium
PTH (parathyroids, sensed by the calcium-sensing receptor, CaSR): released when Ca falls. Raises Ca, lowers phosphate. ↑bone resorption, ↑distal renal Ca reabsorption, ↓renal phosphate reabsorption (phosphaturic), ↑renal 1-α-hydroxylase.
Vitamin D: skin/diet → liver 25-OH (storage form, the one you measure) → kidney 1-α-hydroxylase → 1,25-(OH)₂D (calcitriol, active). Raises both Ca and phosphate via gut absorption.
Quick rule: PTH splits Ca and phosphate in opposite directions; vitamin D moves them together. Reading phosphate alongside Ca tells you which hormone is at fault.
FGF23 (from bone): phosphaturic + suppresses 1,25-vit D — the early driver in CKD-MBD. Calcitonin lowers Ca but is minor in humans.
Corrected Ca: ~45% of total Ca is albumin-bound. Corrected Ca = measured + 0.02 × (40 − albumin g/L). Low albumin → low total but normal ionized (pseudohypocalcemia). When unsure, measure ionized Ca.
pH effect: alkalosis ↑albumin binding → ↓ionized Ca → tetany (e.g., hyperventilation), even when total Ca is normal.

★ Calcium emergencies
Severe / symptomatic hypercalcemia (Ca >3.5 mmol/L, or confusion/arrhythmia): ① IV isotonic saline first — volume repletion + calciuresis ② calcitonin (fast onset, tachyphylaxis in 48 h) ③ IV bisphosphonate (zoledronic acid — onset 2–4 d, durable) ④ denosumab if renal failure/refractory ⑤ treat the cause; dialysis if severe + renal failure. Avoid thiazides (raise Ca); add loop diuretic only once volume-replete/overloaded.
Severe hypocalcemia / tetany (Chvostek, Trousseau, laryngospasm, seizures, prolonged QT): IV calcium gluconate with cardiac monitoring → then oral Ca + active vitamin D (calcitriol). Always check & replace magnesium — hypomagnesemia causes refractory hypocalcemia by impairing PTH secretion/action. Classic setting: post-parathyroidectomy hungry-bone syndrome.

Hypercalcemia

Symptoms "stones, bones, groans, psychiatric moans" — kidney stones/nephrogenic DI/polyuria, bone pain, constipation/abdominal pain/pancreatitis/PUD, confusion/depression; short QT
Causes — 90% are two
Primary hyperPTH commonest in outpatients (often asymptomatic)
Malignancy commonest in inpatients; PTHrP, osteolysis, or 1,25-D (lymphoma)
Others vit D toxicity, granulomatous (sarcoid), thiazides, lithium, milk-alkali, immobilization, thyrotoxicosis, FHH
Work-up
Check PTH first ↑/normal = PTH-mediated (primary, FHH, tertiary); ↓ = PTH-independent (malignancy, vit D)

Hypocalcemia

Symptoms perioral/digital paresthesias, cramps, Chvostek & Trousseau, tetany, laryngospasm, seizures; prolonged QT
Causes
Hypoparathyroidism post-thyroid/parathyroid surgery #1, autoimmune, DiGeorge
Vitamin D deficiency / CKD ↓absorption / ↓1,25-D
Hypomagnesemia functional hypoPTH — refractory until Mg replaced
↑Phosphate tumor lysis, rhabdomyolysis
Other pancreatitis, hungry bone, citrate (massive transfusion), sepsis, pseudohypoparathyroidism
Work-up corrected/ionized Ca, Mg, phosphate, PTH, 25-OH D, creatinine

Hyperparathyroidism — the three types

Primary adenoma 85% (hyperplasia, rarely carcinoma); ↑Ca ↑PTH ↓phosphate. Complications: osteoporosis (cortical/distal radius), nephrolithiasis, osteitis fibrosa cystica / brown tumors
Parathyroidectomy — when (asymptomatic 1°)
Operate if Ca >0.25 mmol/L above ULN, age <50, eGFR <60, T-score ≤−2.5 or fragility fx, nephrolithiasis/marked hypercalciuria
Localize sestamibi + neck US pre-op; watch post-op hungry bone
Secondary appropriate ↑PTH from CKD (↑phosphate) or vit D deficiency (↓phosphate) — treat the cause
Tertiary autonomous glands after prolonged CKD → ↑Ca + ↑↑PTH → cinacalcet or parathyroidectomy
⚠ Don't miss FHH mimics primary but low urine Ca (CCCR <0.01) — surgery won't help

Osteoporosis

Define (DXA T-score) ≤−2.5 osteoporosis; −1 to −2.4 osteopenia; any fragility fracture = osteoporosis regardless of T-score; use Z-score if young/premenopausal
Risk factors age, postmenopausal/estrogen loss, glucocorticoids, smoking, alcohol, low BMI, prior fx, hyperthyroid/hyperPTH, hypogonadism, malabsorption, RA
Secondary work-up Ca, phosphate, PTH, 25-OH D, TSH, testosterone (men), SPEP (myeloma), celiac serology, 24-h urine Ca
Risk tool FRAX 10-yr fracture probability guides borderline cases
Treatment
Treat if T ≤−2.5, hip/vertebral fragility fx, or high FRAX. Ensure Ca + vit D, weight-bearing exercise, fall prevention
Bisphosphonate (1st-line) oral upright 30–60 min empty stomach (or IV zoledronic acid yearly); SE: esophagitis, ONJ, atypical femoral fx, hypocalcemia (replete vit D/Ca first), IV acute-phase reaction
Denosumab RANKL inhibitor, SC q6mo; OK in renal impairment; rebound vertebral fx if stopped/delayed — never just discontinue
Anabolic teriparatide/abaloparatide (severe; avoid if prior radiation/Paget); romosozumab (CV caution) — follow with an antiresorptive
Raloxifene (SERM) vertebral only; ↓breast cancer but ↑VTE
Glucocorticoid-induced prophylax with bisphosphonate if chronic steroids

Paget disease of bone

Mechanism disordered remodeling — excessive osteoclast resorption then chaotic osteoblast formation → enlarged, weak, deformed bone
Presentation often asymptomatic, found on isolated ↑ALP; bone pain, bowing, skull enlargement/frontal bossing, hearing loss (CN VIII), fractures, high-output heart failure; rare osteosarcoma
Labs ↑↑ALP with normal Ca and phosphate; ↑urinary hydroxyproline
Imaging mixed lytic + sclerotic, "cotton-wool" skull; bone scan maps extent
Treatment symptomatic → IV zoledronic acid; track response with ALP

CKD-MBD & exam pitfalls

CKD mineral-bone disorder
Mechanism ↓1-α-hydroxylase → ↓1,25-D; phosphate retention; ↑FGF23 → ↓Ca → secondary hyperPTH
Pattern ↓Ca, ↑phosphate, ↑PTH, ↓1,25-vit D
Treat dietary phosphate restriction + phosphate binders (non-calcium e.g. sevelamer), active vit D (calcitriol), calcimimetic (cinacalcet) lowers PTH & Ca → renal osteodystrophy prevention
Pitfalls
Correct for albumin or measure ionized Ca before calling hypo-/hypercalcemia
Alkalosis tetany from ↓ionized Ca despite normal total (hyperventilation)
Replace Mg hypocalcemia stays refractory until magnesium corrected
Diuretics thiazides ↑Ca (can unmask hyperPTH); loops ↓Ca
