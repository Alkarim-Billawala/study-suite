Pituitary

Endo II · ~2 pp
trigger → answer
Physiology & the anatomy that gets tested
Anterior (adenohypophysis): FSH, LH, ACTH, TSH, Prolactin, GH — driven by hypothalamic releasing hormones via the portal system. Dopamine tonically INHIBITS prolactin (the only one under inhibitory control).
Posterior (neurohypophysis): stores hypothalamic ADH & oxytocin (no synthesis there).
Mass effect: upward growth compresses the optic chiasm → bitemporal hemianopsia; lateral → cavernous sinus (CN III/IV/VI). Headache.
Order of hormone loss in compression ("Go Look For The Adenoma… Promptly"): GH → gonadotropins (LH/FSH) → TSH → ACTH; prolactin often rises (stalk effect).
Stalk effect: compression cuts dopamine delivery → mild prolactin rise (<100–150). Hook effect: a very high prolactin reads falsely low unless the sample is diluted.
★ Pituitary emergency
Pituitary apoplexy — sudden hemorrhage/infarction of a (usually pre-existing) adenoma: thunderclap headache, visual loss, ophthalmoplegia, ↓consciousness. Precipitants: pregnancy/postpartum (Sheehan), anticoagulation, surgery.
The killer is acute secondary adrenal insufficiency. Give IV hydrocortisone immediately, resuscitate, urgent MRI + neurosurgery/ophthalmology; consider surgical decompression.

Prolactinoma / hyperprolactinemia

Presentation women: galactorrhea, oligo/amenorrhea, infertility; men: ↓libido, ED, mass effects (present later/larger)
Confirm prolactinoma prolactin often >250 µg/L; rule out the mimics first
Mimics (mild ↑) pregnancy, dopamine antagonists (antipsychotics, metoclopramide), hypothyroidism (↑TRH), renal failure, chest wall, stalk effect
Treat dopamine agonist (cabergoline > bromocriptine) — shrinks even macroadenomas; surgery only if resistant / progressive visual loss / CSF leak

Acromegaly

Cause GH-secreting adenoma (gigantism if before epiphyseal closure)
Features acral enlargement (shoe/ring/jaw — prognathism), macroglossia, sweating, carpal tunnel, OSA, HTN, T2DM, cardiomyopathy, colon polyps
Work-up
Screen IGF-1 (GH is pulsatile)
Confirm OGTT — GH fails to suppress <1 µg/L
Then pituitary MRI
Treat transsphenoidal surgery 1st; somatostatin analogs (octreotide/lanreotide), pegvisomant (GHR antagonist), cabergoline. Mortality is cardiovascular.

Hypopituitarism

Causes adenoma/treatment, Sheehan (postpartum infarction — failure to lactate, amenorrhea, fatigue), apoplexy, trauma, radiation, infiltration (sarcoid, hemochromatosis, LCH), lymphocytic hypophysitis (checkpoint inhibitors), empty sella
Diagnose low target hormone + low/inappropriately normal trophic hormone; dynamic testing; MRI
Replacement — order matters
Glucocorticoid FIRST then levothyroxine — thyroxine first can precipitate adrenal crisis
Then sex steroids, GH as needed; follow free T4 not TSH (TSH unreliable in central disease)

Posterior pituitary — Diabetes insipidus

Picture polyuria + polydipsia, dilute urine, hypernatremia if water access limited
Central ↓ADH — trauma, surgery, tumor, infiltrative
Nephrogenic ADH resistance — lithium, hypercalcemia, hypokalemia, hereditary
Water deprivation test urine fails to concentrate → give desmopressin: central concentrates, nephrogenic doesn't
Treat central → desmopressin; nephrogenic → treat cause, thiazide, low-salt/protein (± amiloride for lithium)
(SIADH — the opposite; covered under Fluids & Electrolytes)

Other masses & axes

Cushing disease ACTH adenoma — see Adrenal reference (high-dose dex suppresses; IPSS)
Nonfunctioning adenoma commonest macroadenoma; presents with mass effect/hypopituitarism
TSH-secreting adenoma ↑TSH + ↑fT4 (inappropriate) — rare
Craniopharyngioma child/young adult, suprasellar, calcified, bitemporal hemianopsia + DI
Incidentaloma screen for hyper-/hypo-secretion + visual fields if near chiasm

Pitfalls

Central thyroid/adrenal don't trust TSH/normal ACTH — measure free T4 and do dynamic cortisol testing
Hook effect huge prolactinoma with "normal" prolactin — dilute the sample
Always exclude pregnancy before chasing hyperprolactinemia/amenorrhea
Cortisol before thyroxine in any panhypopituitarism replacement
