Upper GI

GI I · ~2 pp
trigger → answer
Physiology & the master switch: alarm features
Acid: parietal cells secrete H⁺ via the H⁺/K⁺-ATPase (PPI target), driven by gastrin, histamine (H2), ACh. Mucosal defense = mucus/bicarbonate + prostaglandins (why NSAIDs ulcerate).
Alarm features ("VBAD-W") that mandate endoscopy over empiric therapy: Vomiting (persistent), Bleeding/anemia, Age ≥60 with new symptoms, Dysphagia/odynophagia, Weight loss.
Dysphagia logic: solids→then liquids = mechanical; solids AND liquids from onset = motility. Transfer difficulty/cough on swallow = oropharyngeal.
Antiemetic targets: 5-HT3 (chemo/GI), D2 (CTZ), H1/M1 (vestibular), NK1 (chemo).
★ Upper-GI emergencies
Perforated peptic ulcer — sudden severe epigastric pain, rigid "board-like" abdomen, free air under diaphragm. Resuscitate, IV PPI + antibiotics, urgent surgery. (Anterior duodenal ulcers perforate; posterior ones bleed from the gastroduodenal artery.)
Boerhaave (esophageal rupture) — forceful vomiting → transmural tear: severe chest/epigastric pain, subcutaneous emphysema, ± left pleural effusion (Mackler triad). CT/contrast study; surgical/emergency management. (Contrast with the mucosal-only Mallory-Weiss tear, which usually self-limits.)

GERD

Typical heartburn + acid regurgitation, worse supine/postprandial
Diagnose clinical if no alarm features → trial PPI + lifestyle (weight loss, head-of-bed elevation, avoid late/large meals & triggers)
Endoscopy if alarm features or refractory
Complications erosive esophagitis, peptic stricture, Barrett esophagus (intestinal metaplasia → adenocarcinoma → surveillance)
Refractory pH-impedance + manometry; consider fundoplication

Dyspepsia & H. pylori

<60, no alarm test-and-treat H. pylori (urea breath / stool antigen) or empiric PPI
≥60 or alarm upper endoscopy
Functional dyspepsia symptoms with normal endoscopy
H. pylori testing rules
Stop PPI ~2 wk before urea breath/stool antigen (false negatives)
Treat quadruple / clarithromycin-based triple therapy; confirm eradication after

Peptic ulcer disease

Duodenal commoner, H. pylori ~90%, pain relieved by food, younger
Gastric NSAIDs, pain worse with food, weight loss — biopsy to exclude malignancy
Zollinger-Ellison gastrinoma — multiple/refractory/distal ulcers, diarrhea, ↑gastrin; MEN1
Complications bleeding (posterior duodenal → GDA), perforation (anterior → free air), gastric outlet obstruction
Treat PPI, eradicate H. pylori, stop NSAIDs

Dysphagia — sorting it out

Oropharyngeal trouble initiating, cough/choke/aspiration — neuromuscular (stroke, Parkinson, myasthenia, ALS)
Esophageal — mechanical (solids first)
Peptic stricture GERD history, progressive
Schatzki ring intermittent solid food (steakhouse) dysphagia
Cancer progressive + weight loss, age/smoking/alcohol (SCC) or Barrett (adeno)
Eosinophilic esophagitis young, atopic, food impaction; rings on EGD
Esophageal — motility (solids + liquids)
Achalasia LES won't relax + aperistalsis; bird-beak on barium, manometry; treat pneumatic dilation/myotomy/botox. Exclude pseudoachalasia (GEJ tumor)
Others diffuse esophageal spasm, scleroderma

Nausea/vomiting & gastroparesis

5-HT3 antagonist ondansetron — chemo/post-op/GI (QT)
D2 antagonist metoclopramide (also prokinetic; tardive dyskinesia), prochlorperazine, haloperidol; domperidone (less CNS)
H1/M1 dimenhydrinate, scopolamine — vestibular/motion
NK1 aprepitant — chemo
Gastroparesis diabetic; vomiting undigested food, early satiety; gastric emptying study; prokinetics (domperidone/metoclopramide/erythromycin) + dietary changes

Pitfalls

Gastric ulcer always biopsy — exclude malignancy
Stop PPI before H. pylori breath/stool testing
New dyspepsia ≥60 scope, don't just treat
Solids+liquids from onset motility (achalasia) — but exclude GEJ cancer
Don't forget Barrett surveillance after chronic GERD
