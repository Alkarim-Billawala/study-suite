Diabetes Mellitus

Endo I · ~2 pp
trigger → answer
Physiology & classification
Insulin (β-cell): anabolic — drives glucose into muscle/fat, suppresses hepatic gluconeogenesis & lipolysis/ketogenesis. Glucagon (α-cell) + cortisol/epi/GH are counter-regulatory.
Type 1: autoimmune β-cell destruction → absolute insulinopenia (anti-GAD/IA-2); ketosis-prone; needs insulin. LADA = slow autoimmune in adults.
Type 2: insulin resistance + relative deficiency; obesity/metabolic syndrome; ketosis-resistant (usually). MODY = monogenic, young, non-obese, autosomal dominant.
Incretins (GLP-1, GIP) from gut amplify glucose-dependent insulin release — the target of GLP-1 RAs and DPP-4i. Secondary DM: steroids, Cushing, acromegaly, pancreatitis, thiazides, antipsychotics.

★ Hyperglycemic emergencies & hypoglycemia
DKA (T1>T2) — glucose usually >14, pH <7.3, HCO₃ <18, ↑anion gap, ketones+. Precipitants: infection, non-adherence, new dx, MI, SGLT2 (euglycemic). Mgmt: ① isotonic saline ② K⁺ — hold insulin if K<3.3, add K when 3.3–5.3 ③ insulin 0.1 U/kg/h ④ add dextrose at glucose ~14, continue insulin until gap closes ⑤ overlap SC insulin before stopping drip.
HHS (T2, elderly) — glucose >33, osm >320, minimal ketones, pH >7.3, profound dehydration + altered mentation; higher mortality. Mgmt: aggressive fluids are the mainstay, cautious K⁺ + insulin, find trigger; watch thrombosis.
Hypoglycemia (<4): oral glucose if alert; IV dextrose / IM glucagon if not. Commonest from insulin/sulfonylureas.

Type 1 vs Type 2

Type 1 young/lean, abrupt, ketosis-prone, autoantibodies+, C-peptide low; insulin always
Type 2 older/obese, insidious, acanthosis nigricans, strong FHx, C-peptide normal/high early
LADA adult, autoantibody+, progresses to insulin
MODY young, non-obese, AD inheritance, non-insulin-dependent

Microvascular complications

Retinopathy NPDR (microaneurysms, dot-blot hemorrhages, cotton-wool, hard exudates) → PDR (neovascularization → vitreous hemorrhage/detachment); macular edema. Annual screen; anti-VEGF / laser
Nephropathy hyperfiltration → microalbuminuria (confirm UACR ×3, exclude transient) → overt; #1 cause of ESRD; ACEi/ARB + SGLT2i
Neuropathy distal symmetric (stocking-glove) — monofilament screen; autonomic (gastroparesis, orthostasis, ED, silent MI); foot care

Macrovascular & vascular protection

Statin all DM ≥40 or with vascular disease; LDL <2.0 / ≥50% reduction
Blood pressure target ~<130/80; ACEi/ARB if albuminuria or HTN
Antiplatelet ASA for secondary prevention (established ASCVD)
Organ-protective agents SGLT2i / GLP-1 RA for CV-renal risk reduction
Lifestyle smoking cessation, weight loss, exercise, foot/eye/renal screening

Pitfalls

Euglycemic DKA SGLT2i — gap + ketones with near-normal glucose; check ketones if acidotic on a flozin
Don't stop insulin in DKA when glucose normalizes — add dextrose, treat to gap closure
K⁺ before insulin if K<3.3 in DKA
Single UACR never diagnoses nephropathy — confirm and exclude UTI/fever/exercise
Sick-day hold metformin + SGLT2i during acute illness/dehydration
